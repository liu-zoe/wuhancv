A Timeline of COVID-19

2019 December 1st

First known patient started experience symptoms 1, 2 .

Dec 8th

First officially reported suspected case of unknown pneumonia in Wuhan 3, 4 .

Dec 30th

- Li Wenliang, an ophthalmologist at Wuhan Central Hospital, warned colleagues about a possible outbreak of an illness that resembled severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS) on WeChat. A few days later, he was arrested and forced to sign a statement in which he was accused of making false statements that disturbed the public order 5, 6, 7 .

- Wuhan Municipal Health Committee issued Urgent notice on the treatment of pneumonia of unknown cause on its Weibo social media account 8.

Dec 31st

The WHO China Country Office was informed of cases of pneumonia of unknown etiology detected in Wuhan City, Hubei 9 .

2020 January 1st

- The Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market was closed for disinfection after two-thirds of the initial 41 hospitalized patients with unknown pneumonia had been exposed to the market 10 .

- Wuhan Municipal Public Security Bureau worte on Weibo that the 8 people who spread "untruthful" information about the pneumonia outbreak was "properly punished by law" 11, 12, 13, 14. After signing the statement, Li was released and went back to work 15 .

Jan 8th

China identified new strain of coronavirus as the cause of the outbreak 16, 17 .

Jan 9th

The first death from the virus occured in a 61-year-old man who frequented the Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market. 17, 18.

Jan 13th

- The WHO labeled the new virus 2019-nCov 19.

- The first confirmed case of 2019-nCov outside China was reported in Thailand. The 61-year-old patient, though a reisident of Wuhan, had not visited the Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market 19, 20. The WHO concluded no clear evidence of human-tohuman transmission given that China has not reported any cases of infection among healthcare workers or contacts of the cases 21.

Jan 15th

- Japan reported its 1st confirmed case of 2019-nCov, the second outside China. Guan Yi, a virologist at University of Hong Kong, said "If there are no new cases in the next few days, the outbreak is over" 22.

Jan 20th

Dr. Zhong Nanshan, head of the health commission team investigating the outbreak, confirmed human-to-human transmission of 2019-nCov in an interview on state-run television and said that "I believe the outbreak will not have the impact on society and the economy that SARS did 17 years ago". Zhong also confirmed that a number of medical workers in Wuhan have tested positive for the virus. Although in a report by the Wuhan Municipal Health Commission from Jan 11, the commission indicated no cases of illness had been found among close contacts, including medical staff. 23, 24.

Jan 23rd

The Chinese government locked down the city of Wuhan to contain the spread of 2019-nCov. All transportation from the city was suspended, including buses, subway, and ferries. Airport and train stations were shut down. 25, 26

Jan 24th

Lancet pulished a study reporting on clinical featuers of patients infected with 2019-nCov. The study analyzed data of 41 patients admitted from December 16th to Jan 2nd, and found the earliest onset date of this cohort of patients to be Decbmer 1st. The study also found that the first person who died from the virus had passed it along to his wife, who had no history of market exposure 1. This early sign that human-to-human transmission was possible came out weeks before the Chinese government officially reported human transmission case 27.

Jan 25th

Chinese Lunar New Year.

Jan 30th

WHO declares 2019-nCov outbreak a global health emergency 28, 29.

Jan 31st

- Major US Airlines announced plans to suspend flights between US and China 30, 31.

- US declares a public health emergency over the 2019-nCov outbreak and barred entry to all foreign nationals who had traveled to China 32.

2020 February 2nd

The first death case outside China was reported in Philippines 33, 34.

Feb 5th

A cruise ship, the Diamond Princess, was quanrantined in Yokohama, Japan after a passenger tested positive. 35, 36.

Feb 6th

Li Wenliang, the doctor who tried to raise the alarm of the virus died after being infected by the same virus 37. In early Jan, Li treated a woman with glaucoma without realizing she was infected. On Jan 10, Li started coughing, and was hospitalized two days later38.

Feb 7th

- #WeWantFreedomOfSpeech trended overtinight and gathered more than 2 million views before it was deleted 39, 40.

- Chen Qiushi, lawyer and citizen journalist, disappeared after posting a vlog on YouTube documenting hospitals in Wuhan 41, 42 . It was later revealed that Chen was forcibly "quarantined" by the Chinese government in Wuhan 43. Days later, another citizen journalist, Fang Bing, was arrested after posting videos of bodies piling up at a crematorium 44.

Feb 8th

A US citizen died of 2019-nCov, which is the first death case of a non-Chinese citizen 45, 46

Feb 11th

- WHO renamed the disease COVID-19 47

- During a live broadcast on the Fox Business Network, anchor Maria Bartiromo suggests China developed COVID-19 as a biological weapon of mass destruction to infect United States diplomats at the White House and in Davos, Switzerland 48.

Feb 14th

- First death case in Europe: A Chinese tourist tested positive died in France 49, 50

- First confirmed case in Africa (Egypt) 51, 52

Feb 17th

Passengers tested negative started disembarking from the quarantined Diamond Princess Cruise ship 53 54

Feb 21st

South Korea saw huge rise in number of confirmed cases 55, 56

Feb 26th

- The WHO reported that the number of new cases outside Chna had exceeded the number of new cases in China for the first time on Feb 25th, after surge of cases in Italy, Iran, and South Korea 57.

- First US case of unknown origin reported in Northern California 58, 59.

Feb 27th

- US stock market experienced sharpest falls since 2018, with the Dow Falling 1,191 points, its largest one-day drop since the 2008 financial crisis 60, 61.

- Nigeria confirmed its first case, which was the first case in sub-Saharan Africa 62

2020 March 2nd

-Several Schools were closed in Washington due to rise of coronavirus cases 63, 64, 65 .

Mar 3rd

- France closed more than 100 schools in virus-hit areas with the highest number of infections 66, 67 .

- Italy announced that it may set up quarantine red zones to contain the spread of the virus 68 .

Mar 4th

Italy closed all schools and universitys until March 15th to contain the virus 69, 70 .

Mar 8th

Italy quarantined Lombardy and 14 other central and nothern provinces 71, 72 .

Mar 9th

Italy extended its quarantine to the entire country 73, 74 .

Mar 11th

- WHO declares the COVID-19 outbreak a pandemic 75, 76, 77 .

- Dow ends 11-year bull market and enters bear market 78, 79, 80 .

- NBA suspends season due to coronavirus 81

- Trump suspended travel from Europe to US (with the exception of UK) 82, 83 .

Mar 12th

- Canadian Prime Minister Trudau starts 14-day self-quarantine after wife tests positive for the virus 84, 85

- Major professional sports leagues in US and Canada announced suspension, canceling or delaying games, including National Hockey League 86 , Major League Soccer 87 , and Major League Baseball 87 .

Mar 13th

- Trump declared national state of emergency 88, 89 .

- The Premier League suspended its season until April 90 .

- Apple closed all Apple Store globally except those in China 91 .

Mar 14th

France and Spain on lock down 92, 93

Mar 15th

- US expands Europe travel ban to include UK and Ireland 94 .

Mar 16th

- Canada closed borders to foreigners, allowing only Canadian citizens, permanent residents and US citizens to enter 95, 96

- Stock market plummets again 97, 98 .

- East coast tri-state (New York, New Jersey, and Conneticut) closed non-essential businesses to curb the spread of COVID-19 99

- San Francisco in lockdown 100, 101

Mar 17th

- COVID-19 now reported in all 50 states and death toll reached more than 100 in US 102

Mar 18th

- US and Canada close their boarder to non-esential travels 103, 104 .

- Ford, GM, and Fiat Chrysler to temporarily close factories in North America 105 .

Note: Events from Jan and Feb are selected from Wikipedia, Financial Times, South China Morning Post, and The Straits Times .
